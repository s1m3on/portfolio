QUIP - Medical Equipment Visibility & Maintenance Platform

A real-time system designed to help hospitals locate, maintain, and extend the life of critical medical equipment -- when access matters most. QUIP was built to support healthcare facilities during the COVID-19 pandemic by providing immediate visibility into medical equipment availability, functionality, and maintenance needs, while connecting hospitals to qualified biomedical engineers when expertise was scarce.

Overview

QUIP is a healthcare operations platform that enables hospitals to track, diagnose, maintain, and procure medical equipment critical to patient care. The system provides real-time insight into equipment status, automated maintenance workflows, and direct access to biomedical engineers -- especially valuable for hospitals without in-house BME teams. Since launch, QUIP has trained and onboarded over 10 biomedical engineers, creating a distributed support network that helps hospitals keep life-saving equipment operational and accessible.

The Challenge

During the height of COVID-19, hospitals faced a critical operational gap:

- No real-time visibility into functional vs. non-functional equipment
- Delayed maintenance responses, leading to idle or unusable assets
- Limited access to biomedical engineers, especially in under-resourced hospitals
- Manual, fragmented processes for diagnosis, repair, and procurement
- High data volume environments, causing performance issues in digital systems

In an environment where minutes could determine patient outcomes, equipment downtime was not an option.

Project Goals

QUIP was designed to:

- Provide real-time visibility into hospital equipment status
- Enable fast diagnosis and resolution of equipment issues
- Connect hospitals to certified biomedical engineers on demand
- Standardize maintenance workflows across facilities
- Support equipment procurement and reuse, especially for used assets
- Remain performant under heavy data loads
- Serve multiple user roles with clarity and precision

The platform needed to function reliably under pressure -- without sacrificing usability.

End Users & System Roles

QUIP supports three primary user groups, each with distinct needs:

LifeBank / QUIP Admin
- Platform oversight and governance
- Equipment availability and approval
- Engineer verification and assignment

Hospital Interface
- Equipment tracking and diagnosis
- Maintenance scheduling
- Spare part requests
- Equipment purchase requests

Biomedical Engineers (BME)
- Registration and verification
- Receiving and responding to service requests
- Providing diagnostic guidance and repairs

The experience was designed to feel cohesive -- while respecting each role's responsibility and urgency.

UX Strategy & Design Rationale

1. Designing for Real-Time Accountability
Medical equipment tracking is mission-critical. Every action -- request, diagnosis, assignment -- needed to be captured instantly and visibly. To support this:

- All activities update in real time
- Status indicators are always visible
- Notifications confirm every major action

This created a shared source of truth across hospitals, engineers, and administrators.

2. Performance-First Interface Design
Given the volume of equipment data and activity logs, system responsiveness was a key risk. To mitigate this, we:

- Limited list and table views to only critical data points
- Deferred secondary details to drill-down views
- Reduced on-screen rendering load

This ensured fast performance even under heavy data usage -- without compromising operational clarity.

3. Guided Self-Diagnosis to Reduce Downtime
One of QUIP's most impactful features is Self Diagnosis, designed to resolve issues before escalation. Process Flow:

- Hospital staff enters error messages or symptoms
- System searches maintenance manuals and documentation
- Automated guidance (authored by BMEs) is displayed
- User confirms whether the issue is resolved

If unresolved, users can immediately:
- Request spare parts
- Book an engineer

This flow reduces unnecessary dispatches and accelerates resolution.

4. Structured Maintenance Workflows
Maintenance scheduling was designed to reflect real hospital operations. Users can:

- Auto-fill equipment details using QUIP ID
- Choose between routine or emergency maintenance
- Select frequency (weekly, monthly, quarterly, etc.)
- Schedule date and time

Each action is confirmed via notification, ensuring transparency and traceability.

5. Engineer Network & Dispatch System
To address the shortage of in-house BMEs, QUIP enables:

- Engineer sign-up and verification
- Admin approval of BME registrations
- Assignment of engineers to hospital requests
- Centralized oversight of all dispatch activity

This system transformed isolated hospitals into connected participants in a shared healthcare support network.

6. Controlled Equipment Procurement & Reuse
Equipment purchasing was intentionally designed as a request-based workflow, not an instant transaction. Key design decisions:

- Removal of direct payment from purchase flow
- Admin approval or decline based on availability
- Notification-driven progression to payment
- All hospital-listed equipment marked as USED by default
- Admins verify quality before approval, ensuring safety and accountability.

Admin Interface Capabilities

The Admin dashboard serves as the operational backbone of QUIP, enabling:

- Viewing all registered hospitals
- Monitoring all equipment across facilities
- Tagging and categorizing equipment
- Reviewing and approving BME registrations
- Managing engineer requests and assignments

The interface prioritizes clarity, speed, and oversight -- supporting critical decision-making.

Design Methodology

Discovery & Research
- Stakeholder interviews with healthcare operators
- Workflow mapping of hospital maintenance processes
- Analysis of pandemic-era equipment bottlenecks

Experience Architecture
- Role-based access control
- Clear separation of hospital, admin, and engineer flows
- System-wide status visibility

Interaction & Interface Design
- Minimalist data tables
- Contextual actions surfaced only when relevant
- Clear error handling and confirmation states

Validation
- Scenario-based testing (emergency vs routine cases)
- Performance testing under large datasets
- Iterative refinement of diagnosis and notification flows

Solution

QUIP delivers a resilient healthcare operations platform through:

- Real-time equipment visibility
- Guided self-diagnosis and escalation
- Structured maintenance scheduling
- Biomedical engineer network and dispatch
- Admin-controlled procurement workflows
- Performance-optimized data presentation

The platform supports hospitals in delivering uninterrupted care -- even in resource-constrained environments.

Impact

Operational Impact

By prioritizing performance, clarity, and real-world workflows, the platform transforms equipment management from a reactive challenge into a proactive, collaborative system -- helping hospitals focus on what matters most: patient care.
